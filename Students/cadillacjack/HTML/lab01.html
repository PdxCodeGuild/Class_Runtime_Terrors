Hunter Stockton Thompson
July 18, 1937 - February 20, 2005

Hunter S. Thompson is a hero of mine. A man of action.
Not only was he an exceptional jounalist, Hunter was also a politician,
as well as an advocate for the use of entheogenic therapies.
"Too weird to live and too rare to die", Hunter was in a class of his own.
When Hunter didn't like what he saw happening in American politics,
he took decisive action and ran for sheriff of Pitken county, Colorado in 1970.
Hunter ran under the slogan of "Freak Power".
The phrase was coined from the mayoral campaign of Joe Edwards in Aspen, Colorado the year before,
which Hunter had written an aricle about in "Rolling Stone".
Hunter is perhaps best known for his "Gonzo journalism" movement.
Gonzo journalism is the method of venturing into the worlds in which you report,
and often participating in the activities of those on whom you're reporting.
Like everything Hunter did, his reporting was fully immersive.

Hunter was born in Louisville, KY on July 18, 1937 and died in Woody Creek, CO on February 20, 2005 at the age of 62 from a self inflicted gunshot wound to the head.

Hunters published works include:
  • "Hell's Angels: The Strange and Terrible Saga of the Outlaw Motorcycle Gangs"

  • "Fear and Loathing In Las Vegas:
    A Savage Journey to the Heart of the American Dream"

  • "The Rum Diary"
A more complete list of his works including articles, photos, and movies can be found here. This is not an exhaustive list of Hunters' work. Hunter wrote extensively for several decades,
which landed him the spotlight in counter-culture for many years.
Few names are as well recognized as Hunter S. Thompson.
Timothy Leary and Paul Staments are among Hunters' counter-culture peers.

My favorite quote from Hunter is from
"Fear and Loathing In Las Vegas"

This is what Hunter had to say in reference to his attorney, Oscar Zeta Acosta:

One of God's own prototypes. A high-powered mutant of some kind never even considered for mass production. Too weird to live, and too rare to die."
